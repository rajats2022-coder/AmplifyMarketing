Scorecard

The weekly report should answer a revenue question.

Clicks, impressions, and cost per click still exist. They just do not get to pretend they are the business outcome.

MetricUseful questionWhat it prevents
Qualified leadsWere these people a real fit for the service and area?Celebrating cheap leads that cannot close.
Booked estimatesDid the inquiry turn into an actual sales opportunity?Confusing form fills with pipeline.
Closed revenueWhat did the campaign help generate?Scaling spend on surface-level ad metrics.
Follow-up speedWas the lead contacted while intent was still high?Blaming ads for slow response.

Scale or cut

A useful report should make the next campaign decision obvious.

The weekly scorecard turns Meta ads into plain owner decisions: increase, revise, pause, or replace.

Timeline

What the first 60 days should clarify.

The early goal is not pretending the account is perfect. It is finding which services, areas, and offers produce usable demand.

Week 1

Campaign plan

Pick services, areas, ad angles, lead form questions, and the reporting scorecard.

Weeks 2-3

Launch and clean up

Start campaigns, remove bad-fit signals, and improve follow-up paths.

Weeks 4-6

Read lead quality

Study the difference between cheap leads and leads that became estimates.

Weeks 7-8

Scale the useful angles

Move more attention and budget toward the services showing revenue potential.

What not to call a result

  • Clicks with no booked estimates.
  • Leads outside the service area.
  • Reports that never mention revenue.

What Amplify wants to prove

  • More qualified inquiries for the services the owner wants.
  • More booked estimates from the campaign path.
  • A clearer decision on what to scale next.
