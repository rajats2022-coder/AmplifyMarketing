Lead quality

Define a quality lead before the campaign starts.

If a quality lead is not defined, cheap form fills can look like progress. Amplify uses service fit, area fit, timing, context, and booking outcome to score lead quality.

Plain rule

A lead is useful when it can become a real estimate for a service the business wants to sell.

Definitions

Every campaign needs shared lead language.

The scorecard keeps owner, agency, and ad account decisions pointed at the same business outcome.

StatusDefinitionNext action
QualifiedFits service area, service type, timing, and job context.Prioritize follow-up and estimate booking.
UnqualifiedWrong area, wrong service, too small, vague, or not reachable.Improve form questions or campaign angle.
Booked estimateQualified lead that became a scheduled sales opportunity.Track close rate and revenue potential.
Closed jobBooked opportunity that became paid work.Use revenue notes to guide scaling.

Owner role

Lead quality is partly campaign, partly follow-up.

The campaign can improve targeting and filtering. The business still has to answer, qualify, estimate, and report what happened.

Track bad-fit reasons

Do not just mark a lead bad. Record why it was bad.

Log booked estimates

Count the leads that became real opportunities.

Report closed revenue

Revenue notes decide whether a campaign deserves more spend.
